Diabetes
Description:
Diabetes is a chronic medical condition that affects how your body regulates blood sugar (glucose). There are three main types of diabetes: type 1 diabetes, type 2 diabetes, and gestational diabetes. While the specific symptoms and severity can vary depending on the type of diabetes, there are some common signs and symptoms to look out for.
Symptoms:
  •  Frequent urination: Increased need to urinate frequently, including during the night.
  •  Excessive thirst: Persistent feeling of being extremely thirsty.
  •  Unexplained weight loss: Losing weight without trying, despite a regular or increased appetite.
  •  Increased hunger: Feeling hungry more often, even after eating.
  •  Fatigue: Persistent tiredness and lack of energy.
  •  Blurred vision: Vision problems, including difficulty focusing or blurred sight.
  •  Slow-healing sores or frequent infections: Wounds take longer to heal, and there is a higher susceptibility to infections.
  •  Tingling or numbness: A sensation of tingling or numbness, particularly in the hands and feet.
Parameters:
  •  Pregnancies: Number of times a person has been pregnant.
  •  Glucose: Plasma glucose concentration measured 2 hours after consuming a glucose solution as part of an oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT). It indicates how the body processes glucose.
  •  BloodPressure: Diastolic blood pressure, measured in millimeters of mercury (mm Hg). It represents the pressure in the arteries when the heart is at rest between beats.
  •  SkinThickness: Triceps skinfold thickness, measured in millimeters. It is a measure of subcutaneous fat and can provide information about body composition.
  •  Insulin: 2-Hour serum insulin levels, measured in micro international units per milliliter (mu U/ml). It represents the amount of insulin present in the blood 2 hours after consuming glucose.
  •  BMI: Body mass index, calculated as weight in kilograms divided by the square of height in meters (kg/m^2). It is used to assess the relationship between weight and height and provide an indication of body composition.
  •  DiabetesPedigreeFunction: A function that quantifies the diabetes history in relatives and their impact on the probability of developing diabetes. It provides a measure of genetic predisposition.
  • Age: Age of the individual in years.
