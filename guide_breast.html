Breast Cancer
Description:
Breast cancer is a type of cancer that develops in the cells of the breasts, usually starting in the milk ducts or the lobules, which are the glands that produce milk. It is the most common cancer diagnosed in women worldwide, although it can also affect men, albeit rarely.
Symptoms:
  •  A lump or thickening in the breast or underarm area: This is one of the most common signs of breast cancer. The lump may feel hard, irregular in shape, and different from the surrounding breast tissue.
  •  Changes in breast size or shape: The breast may appear swollen or noticeably different from its usual size or shape. This can happen in one or both breasts.
  •  Nipple changes: The nipple may become inverted (pulled inward) or change its position or shape. It might also have scaling, crusting, or discharge, which can be clear, bloody, or of another color.
  •  Breast pain or tenderness: While breast pain is not typically a common symptom of breast cancer, some individuals with breast cancer may experience discomfort or tenderness in the breast area.
  •  Skin changes: The skin on the breast may become red, dimpled, puckered (resembling an orange peel texture), or develop ridges or thickened areas.
  •  Swelling in the breast or armpit: Some individuals with breast cancer may notice swelling or a lump in the armpit or lymph nodes near the breast. This can occur if cancer has spread to the lymph nodes.
